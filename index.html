The after-run question

Every run ends the same way. With a guess.

You come off the water and rate the run by feel: it felt connected, the ocean was good, you think you linked more than last time. Or you open the speed trace and count the spikes by hand. Downwind replaces the guess with a number and a picture.

Downwind-native analysis

It speaks the language of the sport.

No pace-per-mile concepts imported from running. Downwind segments every 1 Hz GPS recording deterministically into the units paddlers already think in.

Ride detected

Every bump you catch, found in the trace: when it started, how long you carried it, how fast it peaked. Counted, not remembered.

Link chained

Rides connected into chains. Linking is the skill of downwind, and Downwind measures it directly: chain length, chain frequency, and how both are trending.

Dead water flagged

The stalls between rides, timed and located. Where you lost the ocean matters as much as where you rode it. That's where the next percent comes from.

Same run, session after session

Separate skill from conditions.

The core ambiguity of downwind progression: was that you improving, or was the ocean generous today?

Downwind builds a baseline from the runs you repeat. Every session on the same route is measured against your own history there, so the trend that emerges is skill, not swell.

Evidence-gated coaching

Coaching that can't bluff.

Most fitness apps will tell you a story whether or not the data supports one. Downwind's coaching is mechanically forbidden from doing that.

On GPS alone it describes what happened and how it's trending. Claims about why only unlock when wrist-sensor evidence exists to back them. And when the data is ambiguous, uncertainty resolves to silence, not a guess.

The result, for a sport riddled with luck and feel: an improvement it reports is real. Each session ends with one observable thing to try next run, framed as an experiment, not a diagnosis.
